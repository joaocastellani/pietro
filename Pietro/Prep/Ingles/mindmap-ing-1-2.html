1
Linking Words — Contrast
  • Words: however · but · though · even though · even so · nonetheless
  • Use: to show opposing ideas (mostrar ideias opostas)
however → after ; or . (formal) "Conditions vary; however, everyone can have sensory needs." even though → subordinate clause "Even though braille evolved, the 1829 system is still used."
⚠️ Trap: However needs ; or . before it — never just a comma.
❌ "It rained, however the event continued."
✅ "It rained; however, the event continued."

2
Linking Words — Consequence
  • Words: so · as a result · consequently · therefore
  • Use: to show a result or effect (mostrar resultado ou efeito)
  • so → informal, mid-sentence
  • therefore → formal, new sentence
"Bike lanes were added. As a result, fewer people use cars." → Como resultado, menos pessoas usam carros. "It rained, so we stayed home." → Choveu, então ficamos em casa.
⚠️ Trap: So never starts a sentence alone. Therefore and as a result do.

Linking Words — Addition
  • Words: and · as well as · in addition · furthermore · also
  • Use: to add information (adicionar informação)
"Philadelphia trained workers and created sensory stations." → E também / Além disso "In addition, the city provided sensory bags." → Além disso, a cidade forneceu kits sensoriais.
⚠️ Trap: As well as ≠ and always. Verb agrees with the main subject, not the second element: "The mayor, as well as the council, was present."

4
Linking Words — Cause / Reason
  • Words: because · since · as · due to
  • Use: to explain why something happens (explicar a razão)
  • due to → always + noun/gerund, NOT + clause
"The trains were delayed due to bad weather." → devido a (+ substantivo) "Since the road was closed, we took a detour." → Como / Visto que (causa óbvia)
⚠️ Trap: ❌ "delayed due to it rained" → due to + noun only.
✅ "delayed due to rain" / ✅ "delayed because it rained"

5
Linking Words — Condition
  • Words: if · unless · even if · in case
  • Use: to express a condition (expressar condição)
  • unless = if + not (nunca use "unless + not")
"Buildings will remain inaccessible unless ramps are installed." → a menos que (= if ramps are NOT installed) "Even if it rains, the event will continue." → mesmo se / mesmo que
⚠️ Trap: ❌ "Unless you don't install ramps…" — unless already means "if not". Never double the negative!

Genre: Listicle
  • Definition: list + article — presents info as a numbered or bulleted list
  • Language: concise, direct, objective
  • Key feature: all items must relate to the SAME topic
Typical structure: 1. Introduction (context) 2. Numbered / bulleted list (key points) 3. Conclusion (synthesis) → Model text: Smart City Technologies (pág. 36)
⚠️ Writing tip: Use linking words to connect introduction and conclusion to the list items. Items must NOT cover different topics.

💡 Golden Tips
Tip 1
However needs ; or .
However conecta orações independentes. Exige ponto e vírgula ou ponto antes — nunca só uma vírgula.

❌ "It rained, however the show continued."
✅ "It rained; however, the show continued."
Tip 2
Due to + noun only
Due to é sempre seguido de substantivo — nunca de oração com sujeito + verbo.

❌ "Canceled due to it rained."
✅ "Canceled due to rain."
✅ "Canceled because it rained."
Tip 3
Unless = if not (never "unless + not")
Unless já carrega a negação — nunca duplique.

❌ "Unless you don't install ramps…"
✅ "Unless you install ramps…"
= "If you do not install ramps…"
Tip 4
Listicle: same topic for all items
Em provas, a alternativa FALSA sobre listicle é sempre que "os itens são sobre tópicos diferentes".

Um listicle exige que todos os itens compartilhem o mesmo tema — essa é sua característica central.
Tip 5
Equitable ≠ Equal
Equal = igual para todos (mesma quantidade).
Equitable = justo / proporcional às necessidades.

No contexto de acessibilidade: dar mais recursos a quem tem mais necessidades é equitable, não apenas equal.
Tip 6
So vs. Therefore vs. As a result
Todos expressam consequência — mas diferem em registro e posição:

So → informal, meio da frase
As a result → neutro, início de frase
Therefore → formal, início de frase

So nunca inicia uma frase sozinho.
