## 4. LANGUAGE TOOLS

### 4.1 — Focus on the language

**Estrutura: Linking words**

Linking words are used to connect ideas and indicate the relation between them. They can express comparison, contrast, addition, cause, consequence, condition and more.

| Função | Linking words |
|---|---|
| Condition | if, unless, in case, even if |
| Contrast | however, but, though, even though |
| Consequence | so, as a result, consequently, therefore |
| Addition | and, as well as, in addition, furthermore |
| Cause/Reason | because, since, as, due to |

**Exemplos do material:**

- *"Sensory sensitivities or challenges with sensory regulation are often experienced by individuals with autism, ADHD, PTSD, and other conditions; **however**, everyone can experience sensory needs at any time."* — **however** expresses contrast/consequences
- *"[...] Philadelphia is better positioned to ensure all visitors, residents **and** members of its workforce feel welcomed, supported, **and** have more equitable engagement opportunities [...]"* — **and** expresses addition/consequence
- *"The linking word **so/but** expresses the same idea."* (referência da atividade sobre comic strip)

**Focus on the language — caixa lateral (pág. 34):**

Learn some more linking words and what they express:
- Contrasting ideas: *but, even so, nonetheless, though*
- Addition: *also, as well as, furthermore, in addition*
- Cause and reason: *because, as, since, due to*
- Condition: *even if, if, unless, in case*
- Consequence *and* result: *so, consequently, as a result, therefore*

## 5. WRITING TIME

### 5.2 — Focus on the genre

- **Nome do gênero:** Listicle
- **Características conforme o livro:**

A **listicle** (list + article) is an article that presents information in the form of a list. Such information can be numbered or bulleted. Listicles are very popular because they are concise and easy to read. The text's key points are made clear and objective to the reader.

- **Texto-base presente no material:**

**The history of Braille** *(excerpt)*

[...] Braille, the writing and reading ______ system with raised dots used by ______ people, exists since 1829. Its inventor, Louis Braille, a French blind man, created this tactile alphabet in order to be able to read and write, thus gaining access to education like everybody else. Even though braille has evolved, the 1829 system still constitutes the reading basis for blind and visually impaired people. [...]

*The history of Braille. Inclusive City. Mullen. Available at: http://p.jwed.com/3GN5GF. Accessed on: Apr. 23, 2024.*

**Texto-base para Now I Know / Writing Time:**

Smart City Technologies Can Be Embedded Throughout a City's Infrastructure

[...] Smart cities take in data from every available input – traffic signals, cameras, embedded devices on public transit – then analyze the data with AI and share it through open data pools. [...]

- **Smart pavement** can monitor weather, road conditions, and even road wear and share the information with drivers and traffic controllers.
- **Smart parking** can alert drivers to available spaces, reducing time spent circling and searching for a spot.
- **Smart traffic signals** can respond to traffic conditions and retune themselves to relieve congestion.

[...]

With the help of AI, this awareness can transform passive traffic management into active intelligent transportation systems. [...]

*Urban Mobility and the Future of Smart Transportation. Available at: http://p.jwed.com/4QWDS6. Accessed on: Apr. 29, 2024.*

## 7. FLASHCARDS DO CAPÍTULO

**FC-1**
🔵 Front: What are linking words used for?
🟢 Back: To connect ideas and indicate the relation between them — comparison, contrast, addition, cause, consequence, condition and more. (Conectar ideias e indicar a relação entre elas.)

**FC-2**
🔵 Front: Which linking words express CONTRAST?
🟢 Back: *however, but, though, even though, even so, nonetheless*. Ex: *"Everyone can experience sensory needs; **however**, not all cities are inclusive."* (contraste / porém)

**FC-3**
🔵 Front: Which linking words express CONSEQUENCE?
🟢 Back: *so, as a result, consequently, therefore*. Ex: *"Bike lanes were implemented; **as a result**, many people rely less on cars."* (consequência / portanto)

**FC-4**
🔵 Front: Which linking words express ADDITION?
🟢 Back: *and, as well as, in addition, furthermore, also*. Ex: *"Philadelphia trained its workforce **and** created sensory stations."* (adição / além disso)

**FC-5**
🔵 Front: Which linking words express CAUSE / REASON?
🟢 Back: *because, since, as, due to*. Ex: *"The trains were delayed **due to** bad weather."* (causa / razão)

**FC-6**
🔵 Front: Which linking words express CONDITION?
🟢 Back: *if, unless, even if, in case*. Ex: *"**Unless** public spaces prioritize accessibility, many people will face discrimination."* (condição)

**FC-7**
🔵 Front: What does **disability rate** mean?
🟢 Back: The percentage of people with disabilities in a population. (Taxa de deficiência.) Context: *"Philadelphia leads the 10 largest U.S. cities with a disability rate of 17%."*

**FC-8**
🔵 Front: What does **follow suit** mean?
🟢 Back: To follow someone's example; to do the same as others. (Seguir o exemplo.) Ex: *"Philadelphia hopes to inspire other cities to follow suit."*

**FC-9**
🔵 Front: What does **equitable** mean?
🟢 Back: Fair; providing equal opportunity or treatment. (Igualitário / justo.) Ex: *"Philadelphia ensures more equitable engagement opportunities for all."*

**FC-10**
🔵 Front: What is a **listicle**?
🟢 Back: A list + article — an article that presents information in the form of a numbered or bulleted list. It is concise, easy to read, and makes key points clear. (Artigo em formato de lista.)

**FC-11**
🔵 Front: What does **right-of-way** mean?
🟢 Back: Direito de passagem. The legal right to proceed in traffic before another vehicle or pedestrian. Used in the Urban Mobility Glossary context.

**FC-12**
🔵 Front: What does **set the standard** mean?
🟢 Back: Estabelecer um padrão. To establish a level of quality or achievement that others should follow. Ex: *"Philadelphia Sets the Standard as the First Certified Sensory Inclusive City."*

**FC-13**
🔵 Front: What is ADHD?
🟢 Back: Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder — transtorno de déficit de atenção e hiperatividade. Mentioned as one of the conditions associated with sensory sensitivities in the reading text.

**FC-14**
🔵 Front: Qual é a diferença entre *however* e *but* como linking words?
🟢 Back: Ambos expressam CONTRASTE, mas *however* é mais formal e pode iniciar uma nova oração (após ponto e vírgula ou ponto). *But* é mais informal e une as duas ideias diretamente na mesma frase.

**FC-15**
🔵 Front: What does **yield** mean in the context of urban mobility?
🟢 Back: Dar preferência. To give way to another vehicle or pedestrian. Ex: *"When there is no traffic control signal, drivers must yield the right-of-way to pedestrians."*

**FC-16**
🔵 Front: What does **path** mean?
🟢 Back: Caminho, via. A route or track designed for pedestrians. Ex: *"A path for pedestrians, situated alongside a road."*
