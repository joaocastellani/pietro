## 4. LANGUAGE TOOLS

### 4.1 — Focus on the language

---

#### First Conditional

**Nome da estrutura:** First Conditional

**Explicação:**
The **First conditional** is used to express actions or situations likely to happen in the future. It is formed by **If + Simple present + will/won't + infinitive**.

**Exemplo:** *If I get the chance to interview Thomas, **I will ask** him about the veracity of this piece of news.*

A comma (,) is used after the "if" clause when it starts the sentence. The *if* clause can also come after the main clause, as in "*I **won't read** this news if you tell me it isn't credible.*"

---

#### Second Conditional

**Nome da estrutura:** Second Conditional

**Explicação:**
The **Second conditional** is used for hypothetical situations and their likely consequences. It is formed by **If + Simple past + would/wouldn't + infinitive**.

**Exemplo:** *If you were aware of the content of this video, you **wouldn't watch** it. It's too heavy.*

In informal contexts, the contracted forms **'d** and **wouldn't** are often used. You can also use *"If I were…"* instead of *"If I was"* when referring to hypothetical situations, as in: *If **we** were rich, **I would** travel around the world.*

## 7. FLASHCARDS DO CAPÍTULO

**FC-1**
🔵 Front: What is the First Conditional used for?
🟢 Back: To express actions or situations likely to happen in the future. Formed by: *If + Simple present + will/won't + infinitive*. Ex: "If I get the chance, I will ask him." (Para expressar situações prováveis no futuro.)

**FC-2**
🔵 Front: How is the First Conditional structured?
🟢 Back: *If + Simple present + will/won't + infinitive*. A comma is used when the "if" clause comes first. Ex: "If you check the links, you will find the truth."

**FC-3**
🔵 Front: What is the Second Conditional used for?
🟢 Back: To express hypothetical situations and their likely consequences. Formed by: *If + Simple past + would/wouldn't + infinitive*. Ex: "If she were honest, she wouldn't spread fake news." (Situações hipotéticas.)

**FC-4**
🔵 Front: How is the Second Conditional structured?
🟢 Back: *If + Simple past + would/wouldn't + infinitive*. Ex: "If I were mayor, I would focus on education." Contracts: *'d* and *wouldn't*.

**FC-5**
🔵 Front: What does S.U.R.E. stand for in the infographic?
🟢 Back: **S** = Source | **U** = Understand | **R** = Research | **E** = Evaluate. (Passos para verificar informações antes de compartilhá-las.)

**FC-6**
🔵 Front: What is **fake news**?
🟢 Back: Inaccurate or false information transmitted or published as trusted news content. It can appear in many formats: fabricated stories, manipulated photos/videos, misleading headlines or biased reporting.

**FC-7**
🔵 Front: What is **clickbait**?
🟢 Back: A hyperlink intended to entice the viewer to click on it by employing appealing or exaggerated claims. (Um link sensacionalista criado para atrair cliques.)

**FC-8**
🔵 Front: What is a **hoax**?
🟢 Back: A trick in which someone tells a lie, for example, that there is a bomb somewhere when there is not. (Uma mentira ou engano deliberado.)

**FC-9**
🔵 Front: What is **misinformation**?
🟢 Back: Wrong information often given to someone in an attempt to make them believe something that is not true. (Informação errada dada para enganar alguém.)

**FC-10**
🔵 Front: What does **"lead to"** mean?
🟢 Back: Resultar em. Ex: "Sharing fake news can lead to serious consequences."

**FC-11**
🔵 Front: What is **circular reporting**?
🟢 Back: A phenomenon in which multiple publications report the same piece of false information, making it appear credible. (Várias publicações repetem a mesma informação falsa.)

**FC-12**
🔵 Front: What is an **infographic**?
🟢 Back: A type of communication combining verbal and visual elements (graphs, icons, charts, images). Visually attractive, easy to read and understand. (Tipo de texto que une informação e elementos visuais.)

**FC-13**
🔵 Front: What does **"forward"** mean in the context of news sharing?
🟢 Back: Transmitir / encaminhar. Ex: "Before you choose to forward information, evaluate its source."

**FC-14**
🔵 Front: What does **"coverage"** mean?
🟢 Back: Cobertura (jornalística). Ex: "The news coverage of the event was biased."

**FC-15**
🔵 Front: In the Second Conditional, which form of "to be" is used in the if-clause?
🟢 Back: **Were** — for all subjects (I, he, she, it, we, they). Ex: "If I **were** mayor…" / "If she **were** honest…"

### Summary — FIRST CONDITIONAL

| Categoria / Ramo | Já dado | Lacuna — resposta esperada |
|---|---|---|
| Estrutura positiva | IF + `______` + SIMPLE PRESENT | **Simple present** |
| Estrutura positiva | + `______` + INFINITIVE. | **will** |
| Exemplo positivo | You will `______` / if you pay attention to the details. | **believe it** / [ilegível — resposta parcial] |
| Estrutura negativa | IF + `______` + SIMPLE PRESENT | **Simple present** |
| Estrutura negativa | + `______` + INFINITIVE. | **won't** |
| Exemplo negativo | If you check the links in this news, / You `______` | **won't be fooled** |
| Exemplo negativo | If you open this website, you won't `______` | **find credible information** |

### Summary — SECOND CONDITIONAL

| Categoria / Ramo | Já dado | Lacuna — resposta esperada |
|---|---|---|
| Estrutura — ramo esquerdo | IF + SIMPLE PAST + `______` + INFINITIVE. | **would** |
| Estrutura — ramo direito | IF + SIMPLE PAST + `______` + INFINITIVE. | **wouldn't** |
| Exemplo esquerdo | If they spread gossip about the teacher, they would `______` | **harm her reputation** |
| Exemplo direito | If she were honest, she wouldn't `______` | **spread fake news** |
| Exemplo esquerdo | The school would `______` / if a student shared lies about it. | **take action** |
| Exemplo direito | The kids wouldn't `______` / if `______` | **believe it** / **if they fact-checked it** |
