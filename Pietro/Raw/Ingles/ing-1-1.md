### 3.2 — Take a tip!

*(pág. 15 — caixa "Take a tip!" — em português)*

A técnica *oil on canvas* (óleo sobre tela) é muito comum na pintura e consiste basicamente na utilização de tintas a óleo sobre telas feitas de tecidos como algodão ou linho. Essa técnica, muito versátil e durável, oferece aos artistas uma grande leque de possibilidades na criação de texturas e mistura de cores.

*(pág. 14 — caixa "Take a tip!" — em português)*

A palavra "pictograma" é a combinação de duas palavras gregas: "picto" (do grego "piktos"), que significa "pintando", e "grama" (do grego "grámma"), que significa "letra" ou "caráter". Assim, a junção das duas palavras resulta em "pictograma". Conhecer a origem das palavras é importante, pois não ajuda a compreender melhor o significado delas.

## 4. LANGUAGE TOOLS

### 4.1 — Focus on the language

---

**Estrutura 1: Modal verbs — Should and Have to**

- **Nome da estrutura:** Modal verbs — *should* and *have to*
- **Explicação conforme o livro:**

*Should* and *have to* are modal verbs. They are used before the main verbs in a sentence.

- ***Should*** expresses advice or a recommendation.
  - *You **should** wear a coat. It's cold outside.* (advice)
  - *You should visit MASP: the São Paulo Museum of Art. I heard the museum's collection includes works by renowned artists, such as Van Gogh and Portinari.* (recommendation)
- The negative form of *should* is **should not** and its contracted form is **shouldn't**.
- In the interrogative form, put the subject and *should* in the sentence.
  - ***Should they** go to MASP on foot?*
  - *It's not a good idea! It's too far from the hotel. **They should** go by subway.*

- ***Have to*** expresses necessity and obligation.
  - *In the UK you **have to** drive on the left. It's mandatory.* (It's an obligation.)
  - *The students **have to** finish the survey report today.* (It's necessary.)
  - *The students **don't have to** finish the survey report today.* (It's not necessary.)
  - In the interrogative form, use the auxiliary **do/does**.
  - ***Do** the students **have to** finish the survey report today?*

---

**Estrutura 2: First Conditional**

- **Nome da estrutura:** First Conditional
- **Explicação conforme o livro:**

We use the **First conditional** when we make a prediction or a promise for the future in case a condition actually happens.
In the if-clause, we use **if** plus the **Simple present** to express condition.
In the clause that describes the result or consequence, we use **will ('ll)** followed by the base form of the verb.

Example: *If museums charge expensive admission fees for tickets, access to art **will be** more difficult.*

## 5. WRITING TIME

### 5.2 — Focus on the genre

- **Nome do gênero:** Survey report
- **Características conforme o livro:**

The **survey report** uses formal and impersonal language to describe a specific event. A **survey** is conducted to gather factual information from people, such as their opinions on a certain product or service. The most common way to collect data is through a questionnaire (printed or online) sent to the users. The results of the survey are presented in a report, which includes a summary, an explanation of the methodology used in the survey and its factual results. Also, reports are summed up in a conclusion.

## 7. FLASHCARDS DO CAPÍTULO

**FC-1**
🔵 Front: What does *should* express?
🟢 Back: Advice or a recommendation. Ex: *"You should wear a coat. It's cold outside."* (Você deveria usar um casaco — conselho.)

**FC-2**
🔵 Front: What does *have to* express?
🟢 Back: Necessity and obligation. Ex: *"In the UK you have to drive on the left. It's mandatory."* (No Reino Unido, você é obrigado a dirigir pela esquerda.)

**FC-3**
🔵 Front: Como se forma a negativa de *should* e de *have to*?
🟢 Back: *should* → *shouldn't* (conselho negativo). *have to* → *don't / doesn't have to* (ausência de obrigação). Ex: *"You shouldn't call them now."* / *"The students don't have to finish today."*

**FC-4**
🔵 Front: Qual é a diferença de significado entre *shouldn't* e *don't have to*?
🟢 Back: *Shouldn't* = não é recomendável (conselho). *Don't have to* = não é necessário (sem obrigação). São estruturas diferentes com sentidos distintos.

**FC-5**
🔵 Front: What is the structure of the First Conditional?
🟢 Back: **If + Simple Present** (if-clause) **+ will + base form** (result clause). Ex: *"If museums charge expensive fees, access to art will be more difficult."*

**FC-6**
🔵 Front: When do we use the First Conditional?
🟢 Back: When we make a prediction or a promise for the future in case a condition actually happens. (Previsão ou promessa futura dependente de uma condição real.)

**FC-7**
🔵 Front: What is a *survey report*?
🟢 Back: A text that uses formal and impersonal language to present findings of a survey — data collected through questionnaires — including a summary, methodology and conclusion. (Relatório de pesquisa formal.)

**FC-8**
🔵 Front: What is a *pictogram*?
🟢 Back: A minimalist symbol that represents an idea or a concept. Origins date back to ancient civilizations such as the Egyptian and the Chinese. They transcend language and cultural barriers.

**FC-9**
🔵 Front: What is *oil on canvas* as a painting technique?
🟢 Back: A traditional painting technique using oil-based paints applied on fabric (cotton or linen). It is very versatile and durable, allowing rich color palettes. (Óleo sobre tela.)

**FC-10**
🔵 Front: What artistic movement is Claude Monet associated with?
🟢 Back: Impressionism — an art movement that focuses on landscapes and everyday urban scenes, characterized by rich colors and textures painted outdoors.

**FC-11**
🔵 Front: **City Council** — tradução e contexto
🟢 Back: *Prefeitura.* Usado no texto sobre o Bilbao BBK para indicar o reconhecimento oficial do impacto econômico do festival.

**FC-12**
🔵 Front: **get fired** / **go bankrupt** — o que significam?
🟢 Back: *get fired* = ser demitido(a). *go bankrupt* = ir à falência. Expressões presentes no comic strip da seção Language Tools (pág. 20).

**FC-13**
🔵 Front: What role does the English language play in the art world?
🟢 Back: English is used by artists, critics, collectors and art enthusiasts worldwide to communicate in exhibitions, museums, galleries, festivals and conferences. (Língua franca do mundo das artes.)

**FC-14**
🔵 Front: Exemplo de First Conditional: planta sem água.
🟢 Back: *"If you don't water the plant, it will die."* — If-clause: Simple Present; resultado: *will* + base form.

**FC-15**
🔵 Front: What is the "kilometer-zero policy" in the Bilbao BBK text?
🟢 Back: A policy that prioritized local suppliers from Bilbao and Bizkaia, leading to 72% of employment going to people from Bizkaia and 50% of supplier companies coming from the territory.
